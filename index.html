Dr.Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billon lives

Here's a time line of Dr.Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 -Born in Cresco, Lowa
  • 1933 -Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program know as the "National Youth Administartion"
  • 1935 -Has to stop school and up more money. Works in Civilian conservation Corps, helping starving Americans."I saw how food changed them", he said."all this left scars on me."
  • 1937 -Finishes University and takes a job in the US forestry Service
  • 1938 -marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to budget cuts. inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returns to school study under Stakman, who teaches him about breading pest-resitent plants.
  • 1941- Tries to enroll in the milltary after the oearl Harbor attack, but is rejectedd. instead, the milltary asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Receives Ph.d in genetics and plant pathology
  • 1944 - Reject a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pergnant wife, and flies to mexico to head a new plant pathology program. over the next 16 year, his team breads 6,000 differnt varieties for each major climate on Earth.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season. doubling wheat yields
  • 1970 - Receives the Nobel peace prize
  • 1983 - helps seven Afican countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - becomes a distinguished professor at texas A&M University
  • 2009 - dies at the of 95.

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress." --indian prime minister Manmohan singh
